The Living Tradition

A Sacred Art Born in the Courts of Thanjavur

✦

Tanjore paintings — known as Palagai Padam in Tamil — are among India's most revered classical art forms, born in the splendid courts of the Maratha kings of Thanjavur in the 16th century. Commissioned as objects of divine devotion, they were never merely decorative — they were sacred portals.

Each painting is a meditative act: thick gold foils pressed by hand, precious and semi-precious stones inlaid with devotion, figures rendered in rich mineral pigments on wooden boards prepared with limestone paste. No two paintings are alike. Each carries the breath of its maker.

At Guhan Arts, our master artisans have inherited this lineage across four generations. We invite you to commission a living sacred heirloom for your home.

How Your Painting Is Born

— ✦ —

From your vision to a consecrated heirloom — a journey of devotion, craft, and timeless beauty.

1

Commission & Vision

Share your reference, deity preference, and sacred intentions through our commission form. Our artisan calls you personally to understand every nuance.

2

Design & Approval

Our master artists sketch the composition, select gemstones, and create a detailed design plan. You receive a preview for approval before work begins.

3

Sacred Craftsmanship

40–90 days of meticulous creation: wood preparation, chalk paste layering, pigment painting, 24K gold foil pressing, and gemstone inlay — all by hand.

4

Delivery & Blessing

Each painting is ritually blessed, triple-layered in protective packaging, insured, and delivered to your door with a certificate of authenticity.
